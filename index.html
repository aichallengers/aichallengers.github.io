Who can participate?

The AI project-based competition is open exclusively to middle school and high school students. To be eligible to participate in the competition, students must meet the following criteria:

1. Age Requirement:

- Participants must be currently enrolled as students in a middle school or high school.

- The competition is open to students within the age range of 11 to 18 years.

2. School Enrollment:

- Students must be attending a recognized and accredited middle school or high school.

- Homeschooled students within the middle school or high school age range are also eligible to participate.

3. Individual or Team Participation:

- Students can participate individually or form teams consisting only of middle school or high school students.

- Teams may consist of a minimum of one student and a maximum of 3 students.

4. Supervision and Consent:

- All participants must have the consent of a parent or legal guardian to participate in the competition.

- In the case of team participation, a designated team leader should be responsible for coordinating with the competition organizers.

5. Mentorship:

- While students may seek guidance and mentorship from teachers, parents, or other adults, it is expected that the majority of the project development and implementation is carried out by the participating students themselves.

Notes:

It is important to note that participants must adhere to the competition guidelines, rules, and deadlines provided by the organizers. Failure to comply with the eligibility requirements or any violation of the competition rules may result in disqualification.

The competition organizers reserve the right to verify the eligibility of participants and may request proof of age, school enrollment, or any other relevant documentation.

We encourage middle school and high school students with an interest in AI and its applications to take part in this exciting competition, showcasing their talent, creativity, and problem-solving skills in the field of artificial intelligence.

Please note that specific details, registration instructions, and submission guidelines will be provided separately to the eligible participants.

What do you need to build?

In order to participate in the AI project-based competition, participants are required to build an innovative and functional AI project that showcases their technical skills, creativity, and problem-solving abilities. The competition is open to individuals or teams who are passionate about AI and its applications.

To build a successful project, participants need to:

Choose a Problem or Use Case:

Select a specific problem or use case where AI can be applied effectively. It could be in areas such as natural language processing, computer vision, machine learning, robotics, or any other AI-related domain.

Define Objectives and Scope:

Clearly define the objectives and scope of the project. Outline what the project aims to achieve and the specific features or functionalities it will include. Participants should also consider any constraints or limitations they may encounter during the project development.

Develop AI Algorithms or Models:

Create AI algorithms or models that are relevant to the chosen problem or use case. Participants should utilize appropriate programming languages, libraries, and frameworks to develop their AI solutions. They should also consider the scalability, efficiency, and accuracy of their models.

Collect and Prepare Data:

Identify and collect relevant datasets that will be used to train and evaluate the AI models. Participants should preprocess and clean the data as necessary, ensuring its quality and suitability for the project.

Implement and Test the Solution:

Implement the AI solution using the chosen algorithms and models. Participants should develop the necessary software infrastructure, user interfaces, or any other components required to realize their project. Rigorous testing should be conducted to verify the correctness and robustness of the solution.

Document the Project:

Properly document the project, including the technical details, methodologies used, and any challenges encountered during the development process.

Participants should also provide clear instructions for running and deploying their project.

Create a Demo Video:

Produce a demo video that showcases the functionality, features, and performance of the project. The video should effectively communicate the project's value proposition, highlighting its innovation and practicality.

Prepare Presentation Slides:

Develop a comprehensive presentation slide deck that concisely presents the project's objectives, problem statement, solution approach, implementation details, and results. The slides should be visually appealing, easy to understand, and provide a clear overview of the project.

Participants are encouraged to push the boundaries of AI technology, think creatively, and provide novel solutions to real-world problems. The competition organizers will evaluate the projects based on their technical proficiency, innovation, effectiveness, presentation quality, and overall project presentation.

By building a well-designed and impactful AI project, participants will have the opportunity to demonstrate their expertise, contribute to the field of AI, and compete for recognition and prizes within the competition.

What to submit?

We kindly request you to adhere to the following formal submission requirements for your competition project:

Project Source Code:

Please provide the complete source code of your project. Ensure that the code is well-organized, readable, and properly documented. Include all necessary files and resources required to run the project successfully.

Demo Video:

Create a demo video showcasing how your project works. The video should provide a comprehensive demonstration of the project's functionality, features, and any unique aspects. Make sure the video is clear, engaging, and of sufficient quality to effectively communicate the project's capabilities.

Presentation Slides:

Prepare a presentation slide deck using the provided template. The presentation should be informative, visually appealing, and concise. It should cover key aspects of your project, including an introduction, problem statement, solution approach, implementation details, results, and any other relevant information. Ensure that your slides effectively convey the project's objectives and achievements.

What are the judging rules?

The AI project-based competition will be evaluated based on the following judging rules:

Technical Proficiency (40%):

Demonstration of sound technical knowledge and skills in AI development.

Effective implementation and utilization of AI algorithms, models, and techniques.

Efficient use of programming languages, libraries, and frameworks relevant to the project.

Consideration of scalability, efficiency, and accuracy in the AI solution.

Innovation and Creativity (20%):

Originality and creativity in addressing the chosen problem or use case.

Development of unique features, functionalities, or approaches in the project.

Introduction of novel concepts, techniques, or methodologies in AI implementation.

Problem-Solution Fit (15%):

Clear understanding of the problem or use case being addressed.

Appropriateness of the AI solution in solving the identified problem.

Consideration of potential impact, feasibility, and practicality of the solution.

Results and Performance (15%):

Successful achievement of the project's objectives as stated in the documentation.

Evidence of thorough testing and evaluation of the AI solution.

Demonstrated performance, accuracy, and effectiveness of the implemented solution.

Presentation Quality (10%):

Clarity, coherence, and organization of the presentation slides.

Visual appeal, readability, and effective use of graphics or visuals in the slides.

Concise and comprehensive communication of the project's objectives, methodology, and results.

The judging panel, composed of experts in the field of AI, will review and assess each project based on these criteria. They will evaluate the projects independently and provide individual scores, which will be compiled to determine the final rankings.
